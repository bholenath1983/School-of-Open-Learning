Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

School of Open Learning (SOL), SPPU

1. What is SOL?
SOL (School of Open Learning) is an initiative by Savitribai Phule Pune University (SPPU) that provides flexible, affordable, and distance education for students who cannot attend regular college.
2. Who can apply for SOL?
  • Working professionals
  • Students from rural/remote areas
  • Homemakers
  • Those who couldn't complete formal education earlier
3. What courses does SOL offer?
SOL offers undergraduate (UG) and postgraduate (PG) programs in Arts and Commerce streams.
4. Is SOL recognized by UGC?
Yes, SOL is a part of Savitribai Phule Pune University (SPPU), which is UGC-recognized, making its degrees valid for jobs and higher studies.
5. How do I apply for SOL admission?
  • Visit the official SPPU SOL website.
  • Fill out the SOL admission form online.
  • Submit required documents and pay the fees.
6. What is the fee structure for SOL courses?
SOL provides low-cost education with no tuition fees. Students only pay for exam fees and study materials.
7. How do I access study materials?
  • Study materials are provided in print via study centres.
  • Some resources may be available online through the SOL Student Login portal.
8. What is the SOL Student Login portal?
The SOL Student Login is an online platform where students can:
  • Access study materials
  • Check exam schedules
  • Download SOL Hall Ticket (Admit Card)
  • View SOL Results
9. How are exams conducted?
  • Exams are held at designated centres.
  • Students must carry their SOL Hall Ticket (Admit Card) to appear for exams.
10. How and when are SOL Results declared?
  • SOL Results are published on the official website.
  • Students can check results by entering their roll number.
11. Are there any contact classes or mentorship programs?
Yes, SOL study centres conduct Personal Contact Programs (PCPs) where students can interact with faculty for guidance.
12. Can I pursue SOL along with a job?
Absolutely! SOL is designed for working professionals, allowing them to study without quitting their jobs.
13. Is SOL available in regional languages?
Yes, courses are offered in both Marathi and English for better accessibility.
14. What if I miss the admission deadline?
SOL often has flexible admission cycles, but it's best to check the official website for updates.
15. How do I contact SOL for support?
  • Visit the nearest SOL Study Centre.
  • Contact the SPPU SOL administrative office for queries.
  • Check the official website for helpline numbers/emails.
